III. Short Answer Questions.
1. What is the meaning of verbal communication?
Answer –
When communication takes place with the help of words (spoken are written), it is referred to as the verbal communication. Language plays a very important role in verbal communication.
2. How is verbal communication different from non-verbal communication?
Answer -
Verbal communication uses spoken or written words to convey messages, while non-verbal communication involves body language, facial expressions, gestures, and tone of voice. Verbal focuses on language, whereas non-verbal expresses emotions and supports or sometimes replaces spoken words.
3. List down a few limitations of non-verbal communication.
Answer –
A few limitations of non-verbal communication –
1. It is quit vague and imprecise.
2. Long conversation and necessary explanation are not possible.
3. Some time non verbal may become difficult to understand.
4. What is visual communication? Give two examples of visual communication.
Answer –
visual communication or graphic communication is the third most important method of communication. It can be defined as the transmission of Information and ideas using signs, symbols, photographs, tables, images, drawings, videos and animation.
5. List two limitations of visual communication?
Answer –
Two limitations of visual communication are –
1.Visual communication is costly
2.Complete and detailed information cannot be presented using this method.
6. What is paralanguage in non-verbal communication?
Answer –
Paralanguage refers to the nonverbal elements of the voice. This includes vocal qualities such as pitch, Rhythm, Tempo, resonance control, pace and the use of voice segregates such as “aah”, “um”, etc.
