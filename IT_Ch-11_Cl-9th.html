III. Short Answer Questions
  1. Distinguish between Information and Information Technology:
    Information is processed data that conveys meaning. Data refers to raw facts and figures. IT is the design, development, implementation, and management of computer-based information systems for storing, sending, and retrieving information.
  2. What do you understand by ITeS?
    Information Technology enabled services (ITeS) refer to outsourcing of services that can be enabled with IT, e.g., call centers, bank office services, medical transcription, data management, and HR services.
  3. Meaning and benefits of outsourcing:
    Outsourcing is hiring external entities to perform tasks previously done in-house. Benefits: reduces costs, increases efficiency, focuses on core tasks, provides expert services, advanced technology, and flexibility.
  4. What is BPO? Name some important BPO companies in India:
    Business Process Outsourcing (BPO) contracts external providers to handle business processes. Important BPOs: TCS, Infosys BPM, Wipro BPO, Tech Mahindra, HCL Technologies.
  5. What is IT-BPM sector? Its impact on Indian economy:
    IT-BPM includes IT services and business process management. Benefits: job creation, export growth, foreign investment, support startups, enhance productivity, and boost GDP.
  6. Three main types of IT-BPM organizations in India:
    (i) Multinational Companies (MNCs): global firms providing IT/BPM services.
    (ii) Indian Companies: headquartered in India, operating locally & internationally.
    (iii) Global In-House Centers (GICs): serve parent companies and external clients.
  7. Difference between BPO and BPM sectors:
    BPO: outsourcing specific business tasks. BPM: optimizes processes using technology for efficiency.
  8. Examples of IT in daily life:
    Computers, smartphones, smart appliances (washing machines, refrigerators, microwaves, ATMs), and microchips with software help manage personal and business data.
  9. Use of IT in Banks:
    IT enables online transactions, ATMs, credit/debit cards, capital market transactions, faster processing, improved security, and easy accessibility.
  10. Use of IT in Education:
    Smartboards, projectors, online tests, virtual labs, remote learning via SWAYAM/DIKSHA, assistive technologies, digital attendance, and report cards.
  11. Role of IT in Healthcare:
    Supports EHR, telemedicine, online appointments, diagnostic tools (ECG, CT), healthcare apps, wearables, and hospital management systems for efficiency and accessibility.
  12. Use of IT in Government services:
    Enables e-governance, online document applications (Aadhaar, PAN, passport), digital payments, grievance redressal via apps (UMANG, Digital India), transparency, and data management.
