III. Short Answer Questions.

1. Distinguish between Information and Information Technology.
Answer –
Information is the result of data processing. Data refers to the raw facts and figures, which are processed to get information. For example marks obtained by each student in a class test is factual data. Information Technology (IT) is defined is the design, development, implementation and management of Computer-Based information system for storing, sending and retrieving information.

2. What do you understand by the term ITeS ?
Answer -
The term Information Technology enable services or ITES refers to the Outsourcing of processes or services that can be enable with information technology and can be offered from an IT enabled remote location. Most popular services included in ITeS are Call Centre services, bank office services, medical transcription, data centers, database management and human resource services etc.

3. What is the meaning of the term outsourcing? How is it beneficial for the business organizations?
Answer - Outsourcing is the business practice of hiring an external individual, company, or third-party service provider to perform tasks, handle operations, or provide services that were previously done in-house by the company’s own employees. Outsourcing reduces costs, increases efficiency, and allows businesses to focus on core tasks. It provides access to expert services, advanced technology, and flexible resources. This helps companies save time, improve productivity, and manage operations more effectively and competitively.

4 What do you understand by BPO? Give names of some important BPO companies in india.
Answer -
Business Process Outsourcing (BPO) is a type of outsourcing where responsibility of performing a particular business process is contracted to an outside service provider. This allows companies to focus on their core business processes. BPO also comes under IT enabled Services as IT plays a key role in optimizing the performance and delivery of the outsourced processes. Here are some important BPO (Business Process Outsourcing) companies in India: 1. Tata Consultancy Services (TCS) 2. Infosys BPM 3. Wipro BPO 4. Tech Mahindra 5. HCL Technologies.

5. What is the IT-BPM sector? How is it helping the Indian economy?
Answer -
The IT-BPM sector stands for Information Technology and Business Process Management. It includes services like software development, IT consulting, business process outsourcing (BPO), data analytics, cloud computing, and customer support. The IT-BPM sector boosts India's economy by creating jobs, increasing exports, attracting foreign investment, supporting startups, enhancing productivity, and contributing significantly to GDP growth.

6. What are the three main types of organizations that constitute the IT-BPM sector in India?
Answer -
The three main types of organizations in the IT-BPM sector in India are:
(i).Multinational Companies (MNCs): Global firms that operate in India to access skilled talent and cost advantages.They offer IT and BPM services to clients worldwide.
(ii).Indian Companies:
These companies have their origins in India.Most of them have their headquarters in india while having offices in many international location.
(iii).Global In-House Centers (GICs):GIC organizations cater to the needs of their parent company only and serve external clients.

7. How is BPO sector different from the BPM sector?
Answer -
The BPO (Business Process Outsourcing) sector focuses mainly on outsourcing specific business tasks, like customer support or data entry, to third-party service providers. The BPM (Business Process Management) sector goes beyond outsourcing — it includes analyzing, improving, and optimizing business processes for better efficiency and performance, often using technology and automation.

8. Give some examples of the use of IT in our everyday life.
Answer -
In our everyday life, we use computers and smart phones to store all the information that we need for our personal and business use like information that is important for our work, appointment schedules and list of contacts. Most of the gadgets like washing machine, refrigerator, car, microwave oven, geyser, ATM machines etc., are controlled by an embedded microchip and software.

9. Write a short note on the use of IT in Banks.
Answer -
Today, banking relies heavily on IT to offer efficient and convenient services. Customers can check balances, make deposits, and manage overdrafts online. Transactions are processed swiftly through credit/debit cards and smart banking. ATMs allow easy cash withdrawal, deposits, and balance inquiries without visiting the bank. Online platforms also support capital market transactions and financial analysis. Overall, IT has transformed banking by improving service speed, security, and accessibility, making financial operations smoother for both banks and customers.

10. How is education in our country getting benefitted from the use of IT?
Answer -
Education in our country is greatly benefitting from the use of Information Technology (IT) in the following ways:
(i). IT in Schools and Classrooms: Smartboards, projectors, and digital tools make learning interactive. Teachers use multimedia to explain complex concepts easily. Digital attendance, report cards, and online tests improve school management. Virtual labs and simulations enhance science and technical learning.
(ii). IT Has Made Education Accessible to All:Online platforms like SWAYAM, DIKSHA, and YouTube offer free quality content. Students in remote and rural areas can access learning anytime, anywhere.Mobile apps and internet connectivity support self-paced learning. Assistive technologies help children with special needs to learn effectively.

11. Describe the role of IT in the healthcare sector.
Answer -
Information Technology plays a vital role in modern healthcare. It supports Electronic Health Records (EHR), telemedicine, and online portals for appointments and reports. IT enables the use of advanced diagnostic tools like ECG, EEG, and CT scans, which rely on computer systems for accurate monitoring and analysis. Healthcare apps, wearables, and hospital management systems further improve patient care, making healthcare more accessible, efficient, and technology-driven.

12. How the use of It is helping the government to deliver various government services to the citizens?
Answer -
Information Technology has made government services more efficient, transparent, and accessible. Citizens can apply for documents like Aadhaar, PAN, and passports through online portals. Digital platforms enable easy payment of taxes and bills. E-governance apps like UMANG and Digital India offer access to schemes, certificates, and grievance redressal. IT ensures transparency by allowing real-time tracking of services. It also helps the government manage data effectively, reaching even remote areas and improving service delivery.
