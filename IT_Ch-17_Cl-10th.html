III. Short Answer Questions:

1. What do you understand by the term “workplace accidents”?

Answer- A workplace accident is an unplanned event that results in injury to a personnel or damage to the worksplace property.
2. Explain the following types of accidents:
a. Repetitive Strain Injury
b. Cuts and lacerations
c. Slips, trips and falls

Answer-
a. Repetitive Strain Injury-
It is not just a problem for those of us who regularly use keyboards in our work. In fact, it can result from any repetitive motion of the joints.
b. Cuts and lacerations-
Many workplaces have tools and implements which can give their user a penful cuts. The most common causes of these cuts and laceration includes poor training, inadequate safety procedures and failing to wear the proper protection.
c. Slips, trips and falls-
Slips → loss of balance due to low friction Example: wet floor, oil, loose dust.
Trips → foot hits an obstacle.
Example: uneven floor, wires, broken tiles, school bags in corridors.
Falls → the result of a slip or trip.
Example: falling from stairs, ladders, playground equipment.

3. What is a workplace emergency? Name any five workplace emergencies.

Answer- A workplace emergency is an unforeseen situation that threatens your employees, customers, or the public; disrupts or shuts down your operations; or causes physical or environmental damage.Some examples of emergencies are - epidemics, fires, floods, hurricanes, tornadoes, release of toxic gases, chemical spills, radiological accidents, explosions, civil disturbances, and workplace violence resulting in bodily harm and trauma etc.
4. How would you handle a workplace emergency? Explain.

Answer- A workplace emergency can be handled by staying calm, alerting others, following the emergency response plan, evacuating safely, providing first aid, contacting emergency services, and waiting at the assembly point until the situation is declared safe.
5. What do you understand by “Emergency Action Plan”?

Answer- An emergency action plan is a written set of instructions that outlines what workers and others at the workplace should do in an emergency. In other words, an emergency action plan covers designated actions employers and employees must take to ensure employee safety from fire and other emergencies.
