III. Short Answer Questions.

1. What is communication? Give an example.
Answer -
Communication means the exchange of ideas and information through speech, writing, gestures, or other means. For example – A teacher communicates with students to share knowledge and information.

2. What is the communication cycle?
Answer -
Communication is a two-way process where information is transmitted from a sender to a receiver. This process continues with feedback, forming a cycle called the communication cycle.

3. What is the importance of communication? Write any three points.
Answer -
• Convey Information: Share facts, ideas, and updates clearly.
• Influence Others: Persuade or motivate people positively.
• Express Feelings: Share emotions and build connections.

4. What is verbal communication? Give an example.
Answer –
Verbal communication is passing messages using spoken or written words. Example: Face-to-face conversations, phone calls, letters, emails.

5. How does oral communication differ from written communication?
Answer -
Oral communication means speaking directly (face-to-face or calls). It’s quick with instant feedback. Written communication (letters, emails, texts) is slower but can be stored for future reference.

6. What is non-verbal communication? Give an example.
Answer -
Non-verbal communication happens without words—through expressions, posture, gestures, and body language. Example: Thumbs-up, shrugging shoulders, eye contact.

7. What is visual communication?
Answer -
Visual communication uses pictorial or graphic representation of information. Examples: Signboards, maps, pictures, charts, graphs, TV programs, hoardings.

8. What is meant by perspective?
Answer -
A perspective is a particular attitude or way of thinking about something. For example, if you believe your father is strict, you may misunderstand his normal words as scolding.

9. Name any four factors that affect our perspective on communication.
Answer –
1. Language differences
2. Visual perception
3. Past experience
4. Prejudice

10. What is prejudice? How does it affect our perspective of communication?
Answer -
Prejudice is having fixed ideas, e.g., “No one in my class likes me.” Such thoughts prevent open communication.
