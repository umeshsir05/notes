IV. Short Answer Questions.

1. Define the terms - entrepreneur and entrepreneurship.
Answer –
An entrepreneur is a person who is self-employed, is willing to take a calculated risk and brings in a new idea to start a business.
Entrepreneurship is the process of developing a business plan, launching and running a business using innovation to meet customer needs and to make a profit.

2. What are the different types of product based businesses?
Answer –
There are two types of product base business –
1. Manufacturing business - These are the business that make a product and sell it. Products are usually made in factories with the help of workers.
2. Trade businesses - These businesses buy and sell a product. They also transport the product from the factory to the warehouse and then finally to shops near the customers.

3. What is a hybrid business? Give an example.
Answer –
A hybrid business is the one where the business is doing both that is selling both products and services. For example, Sports academies.

4. What are the different types of business activities in entrepreneurship?
Answer –
The different types of business activities are:
1. Product business sells physical or digital goods to customers.
2. Service business provides expertise or assistance instead of products.
3. Hybrid business combines both, offering products along with services, like a company selling software and providing maintenance or support.

5. List some benefits of entrepreneurship.
Answer –
The various benefits of entrepreneurship include the following:
● Entrepreneurship allows you to do something that you like using your hobbies and skills.
● An entrepreneur works for himself and not for someone else.
● An entrepreneur earns money for himself. Entrepreneurship gives him freedom to decide how much money he wants to earn and how he may want to earn it.
● Even though there are risks in entrepreneurship, it allows you to decide how much risk you want to take. Usually, bigger risk means more profit.

6. List some differences between the wage employment and the entrepreneurship.
Answer –
Entrepreneurship and wage employment differ in several ways, including:
1. Risk: Entrepreneurship involves calculated risk-taking, while wage employment is generally less risky.
2. Control: Entrepreneurs have more control over their work environment and can adapt production processes to maximize returns. Employees are constrained by the organizational and wage structure of their employer.
3. Lifestyle: Entrepreneurship involves establishing and running a business, while wage employment involves working for a company.
4. Earnings: Entrepreneurs' earnings are the profits of their business, while employees receive their income at a fixed pay rate.
5. Autonomy: Entrepreneurship provides more autonomy, while wage employment often limits personal autonomy.
6. Work-life balance: Wage employment offers a better work-life balance, while entrepreneurs may need to work on their business day in and day out.
7. Career growth: Wage employment offers structured career growth. Career progression is less defined for an entrepreneur.
