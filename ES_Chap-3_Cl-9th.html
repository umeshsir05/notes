IV. Short Answer Questions.
1. What is self-management?
Answer –
Self-management is the ability to regulate one’s emotions,thoughts and behaviour effectively in different situation. We also call it self-control our self-regulation.

2. What is meant by the self-management skills?
Answer –
The set of skills that help us to manage ourselves in different situations are known as the self-management skills. Self-management skills include self-awareness, self-control, self-confidence, problem solving, self-motivation, personal hygiene and grooming, positive thinking, team work, time management and goal setting.

3. Name any three self-management skills.
Answer –
Three Self-management skills are:
1. Self-awareness: Knowing yourself as an individual including your values, likes, dislikes, strengths and weaknesses.
2. Self-control: Ability to control your behaviour, discipline, etc.
3. Self-confidence: Believing in yourself that you can do any task that is given to you and not scared of taking risks.

4. What do you understand by positive thinking as a self-management skill?
Answer –
Positive thinking as a self-management skill means staying optimistic and confident, even in difficult situations. It helps in reducing stress, improving focus, and making better decisions. By thinking positively, individuals can manage emotions, stay motivated, and handle challenges effectively, leading to personal growth and better mental and emotional well-being.

5. What is the role of strength and weakness analysis in self-management?
Answer –
Strength and weakness analysis is an essential aspect of personality development. It involves understanding your strengths, weaknesses, values, beliefs, and motivations. It helps in making informed decisions, improving relationships, and achieving personal and professional goals. It converts weaknesses into strengths and strengths into exceptional performance.

6. What is the difference between interests and abilities?
Answer –
Interests are the things or activities that we enjoy doing. For example, activities done at school or home in your free time that make you happy. There are activities you are curious about or do even if no one asks you to do them.
Ability on the other hand is an acquired or natural capacity that enables an individual to perform a particular task with considerable proficiency.
