IV. Short Answer Questions:
  1. What do you mean by hardware and software?
    Answer: Hardware are the physical parts of a computer system, e.g., keyboard, mouse, monitor, printer, CPU, speakers. Software is a collection of programs, procedures, and documentation that instructs the hardware to perform tasks, e.g., MS Windows, Linux, MS Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Photoshop.
  2. What are input devices? Give examples.
    Answer: Devices that help enter data and instructions into the computer. Examples: Keyboard, mouse, scanner, web camera, microphone, joystick, light pen, trackball, MICR, OCR, OMR, BCR.
  3. What are output devices? Give examples.
    Answer: Devices used to display or communicate processed data. Examples: Monitor, printer, speaker, headphones, plotter.
  4. What are storage devices? Give examples.
    Answer: Devices used to store data permanently. Examples: Hard disk, floppy disk, CD, DVD, Blu-ray disc, pen drive.
  5. What are the two main types of computer memory?
    Answer:
    • Primary memory (RAM, ROM) – fast and volatile, stores active data temporarily.
    • Secondary memory (Hard drives, SSDs) – slower but permanent, stores files and programs long-term.
  6. What is the use of RAM and ROM?
    Answer:
    • RAM: Temporarily stores data and instructions while the computer is running.
    • ROM: Permanent memory storing start-up instructions that cannot be modified easily.
