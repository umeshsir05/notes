IV. Short Answer Questions:
1. What do you mean by ICTs?
Answer -
The term ICT (Information and Communication Technology) refers to a diverse set of technologies, tools and resources that help us to create, stores, manage and share information.

2. What is the use of ICTs in the education field?
Answer-
ICT enhances education by making classes interactive and enjoyable. It uses media like images and videos to boost students' memory and understanding. ICT tools simplify complex topics for teachers and offer remote access to abundant learning resources anytime, anywhere, benefiting unlimited learners.

3. How do ICTs help in the healthcare sector?
Answer –
ICT has improved healthcare by enabling efficient communication, remote consultations, and telemedicine. It ensures accurate digital health records and patient information management for effective diagnosis and treatment. ICT also supports medical research, new treatment development, and handles administrative tasks like appointment scheduling, medical record management, and billing processes.

4. How can radio and television help students in their studies?
Answer –
Radio and television play a vital role in education by broadcasting lectures, educational programs, and useful information. These media support students by providing accessible learning resources, enhancing understanding, and reaching learners in remote areas where schools or teachers may be limited.

5. What is telemedicine?
Answer-
Telemedicine is the use of ICT to provide medical services remotely using electronic communication.

6. What role does the ICT play in the field of business and commerce?
Answer –
Use of ICT enabled digital tools and platforms in business and Commerce is helping to promote products and service, engage with customers, analyse market data and facilitate online transaction etc.

7. What is a smartphone?
Answer –
A smartphone, a popular ICT too, is a multipurpose mobile computing device that has features of mobile phones as well as a computer. Having a rechargeable battery and touch screen features, it allows user to make voice and video calls; send text messages and emails; capture images and videos play games; play media and connect to internet service through cellular, Bluetooth and Wi-Fi networks.

8. Name some default apps which come preinstalled on all smartphones?

Answer –
Some default apps are phone, camera, calendar, Mail, photos, clock, maps, messages, web browser, music, Google play store and App store.

9. What is Bluetooth? What is its use?
Answer –
Bluetooth is a short-range wireless technology which helps you connect with other Bluetooth enable devices that are within 30 feet of where you are. Once connected, you can send messages and share files.

10. What are the different parts of a smartphone screen?
Answer –
A smartphone's main parts include the touchscreen for display and touch input, the status bar and notification bar for alerts, app icons for launching applications, navigation buttons for movement, and the home screen as the central access point for functions.
