III. Short Answer Questions.

1. What is the meaning of stress in our day-to-day life?
Answer -
Stress can be defined as emotional, mental, physical and social reaction to any perceived demands or threads. These demands or threads are called the stress causing agents or stressors.
---

2. Describe some ways of managing stress.
Answer -
1. Time management – Proper time management is one of the most effective stress relieving techniques.
2. Physical exercise and fresh air – Walk, run, or play outside for energy and relaxation.
3. Positivity – Think good, stay hopeful, and spread happiness in daily life.
4. Organising academic life – Keep notes, timetable, and assignments neatly for better learning.
5. Healthy diet – Eat fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and drink enough clean water.
6. Sleep – Take proper rest at night to refresh body and mind daily.
7. Spending holidays with family and friends – Enjoy time together, laugh, and create happy memories.
---

3. What are stressors? Give one example.
Answer -
Stressors are events, situations, or conditions that cause stress.
Example: 1. Your exams are two close but you feel unprepared.
2.You are experiencing a loss of someone’s closed in the family.
---

4. What is self-management? Which two things are essential for developing self-management skills?
Answer -
Self management or ‘self control’ is the ability to control one’s emotions, thoughts, and behaviour effectively in different situations. Two essential things are personality and abilities.
---

5. What is the meaning of emotional intelligence?
Answer -
Emotional intelligence is the ability to identify and manage one’s own emotions, as well as the emotions of others. It is generally said to include at least three skills - emotional awareness, Harnessing emotions and managing emotions.
---

6. List any two characteristics needed to become an independent worker.
Answer -
Here are two characteristics of the person who can work independently-
1.Becoming self-awareness, self monitoring and self correcting.
2. Taking the initiative rather than being told what to do.
