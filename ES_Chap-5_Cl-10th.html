III. Short Answer Questions:
  1. What is the meaning of stress in our day-to-day life?
    Answer: Stress can be defined as emotional, mental, physical, and social reaction to any perceived demands or threats. These demands or threats are called stress-causing agents or stressors.
  2. Describe some ways of managing stress.
    Answer:
    • Time management: Proper time management is one of the most effective stress-relieving techniques.
    • Physical exercise and fresh air: Walk, run, or play outside for energy and relaxation.
    • Positivity: Think good, stay hopeful, and spread happiness in daily life.
    • Organising academic life: Keep notes, timetable, and assignments neatly for better learning.
    • Healthy diet: Eat fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and drink enough clean water.
    • Sleep: Take proper rest at night to refresh body and mind.
    • Spending holidays with family and friends: Enjoy time together, laugh, and create happy memories.
  3. What are stressors? Give one example.
    Answer: Stressors are events, situations, or conditions that cause stress.
    Example: Exams approaching when you feel unprepared; loss of a loved one.
  4. What is self-management? Which two things are essential for developing self-management skills?
    Answer: Self-management is the ability to control one’s emotions, thoughts, and behaviour effectively in different situations. Two essential things are personality and abilities.
  5. What is the meaning of emotional intelligence?
    Answer: Emotional intelligence is the ability to identify and manage one’s own emotions, as well as the emotions of others. It includes emotional awareness, harnessing emotions, and managing emotions.
  6. List any two characteristics needed to become an independent worker.
    Answer:
    1. Becoming self-aware, self-monitoring, and self-correcting.
    2. Taking initiative rather than being told what to do.
