III. Short Answer Questions:

1.What do you understand by the term 'workplace hazards'?
Answer-
A hazard is any source or thing that can cause potential damage, harm or adverse health effects on something or someone.It is a thing, situation, or state of mind etc., that has the potential to cause harm, damage, injury or ill health.

2.Give some examples of the workplace hazards.
Answer-
Some examples of the workplace hazards are-
1.An object that could fall from a height (potential or gravitational energy),
2.A run-away chemical reaction (chemical energy),
3.The release of compressed gas or steam (pressure; high temperature),
4.Entanglement of hair or clothing in rotating equipment (kinetic energy),
5.Contact with electrodes of a battery or capacitor (electrical energy).

3. List five general precautions that you should take to maintain safety at the workplace?
Answer-
Five general precautions are-
1.Your safety is your personal responsibility. Always follow the correct procedures. Never take shortcuts.
2.Take responsibility and clean up if you made a mess. Clean and organize your workspace.
3.Ensure a clear and easy route to emergency exits and equipment.
4.Be alert and awake on the job. Be attentive at all times to your work surroundings.
5.When in doubt, contact your supervisor or manager for instruction, guidance, or training.

4. Differentiate between physical and chemical hazards.
Answer-
Physical hazards can be caused by continuous exposure to loud noise, radiation, extreme temperatures and pressure etc. Certain Chemicals are harmful at some levels when mixed with other chemicals.Chemicals hazards are very common in apparel and textile industry while at the time of dyeing and printing.

5. What do you understand by the biological hazards?
Answer-
Biological hazards include things of biological origin that pose a risk to the workers. Sources of biological hazards may include bacteria, viruses, insects, plants, birds, animals, and humans themselves. These sources can cause a variety of adverse health effects among workers. Some examples of biological hazards are as follows:
a. A poisonous snake is an example of a biological hazard as snake bite can put a person's life in danger.
b. Infections caused by microorganisms like flu, tuberculosis, and more recently COVID-19. An infected worker can pass on the infection to his fellow workers.
c. Waste generated in hospitals and research facilities may contain disease-causing organisms that could infect the people who are working at the waste disposal site.

6. What is meant by the psychological hazards? Answer-
A psychosocial hazard or work stressor is any occupational hazard that affects the psychological a physical well-being of workers, including their ability to participate in a work environment among other people.
These could be due to a feeling of job insecurity, long working hours, lack of enthusiasm towords work, frustration about not being allowed to deliver quality product due to pressure of quantitative production, harassment at work place and workplace stress and pressure of meeting deadlines etc.

7. List three things that one should do to maintain a healthy lifestyle?
Answer-
Three things that one should do to maintain a healthy lifestyle are-
1.Eat Sensibly:
Eat balanced, nutritious food and avoid junk food. Have a filling breakfast, choose healthy meals, and snack on fruits to stay energetic and avoid sluggishness.
2.Drink Plenty of Water:
Drink 6–8 glasses of water daily. Proper hydration prevents drowsiness and fatigue; fruits also help fight dehydration.
3.Keep Clean & Exercise Regularly:
Maintain hygiene, use hand sanitizer, exercise regularly, and practice yoga for overall health.
