III. Short Answer Questions:

1.What do you understand by the term 'Occupational Health and Safety'?
Answer -
Occupational health and safety (OHS) relates to HEALTH, SAFETY, and welfare issues in the WORKSPACE. It includes the laws, standards, and programs that are aimed at making the workspace better and the ensuring health and safety for workers, customers and other stakeholders.

2.Differentiate between class A and class C fires?
Answer -
Class A fires: These are the fires caused by combustible materials or flammable solid, such as wood, papers and fabric.
Class C fires: Fires that are caused by faulty electrical circuits and equipments are classified as the electrical fires.

3.Why should we not use water to extinguish an electric fire?
Answer -
Water contains ions that conduct electricity, so pouring it on an electrical fire can cause electric shock. It may also create short circuits, increasing heat and spreading the fire further. Therefore, only non-conductive extinguishers like CO₂ or dry powder should be used.

4.How do carbon dioxide based fire extinguishers work?
Answer -
We know that oxygen is essential to start and sustain fire. A fire cannot keep burning if its oxygen supply is cut off. The CO₂ extinguisher works by cutting off oxygen from the fire.
