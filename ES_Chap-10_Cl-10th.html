III. Short Answer Questions.

1. What is meant by sustainable development?
Answer-
Sustainable development is the development that satisfies the needs of the present without compromising the capacity of future generations, guaranteeing the balance between economic growth, care for the environment and social well-being.

2. How sustainable development helps manage the climate change?
Answer-
It helps to manage the climate change: Climate change can be mitigated through sustainable development practices. These practices seek to reduce the use of fossil fuel based sources of fuel such as petrol, diesel, natural gas and coal. Fossil fuels as sources of energy are unsustainable since they will be depleted at some time. Their burning is also responsible for the emission of greenhouse gases, and consequent global warming and climate change.

3. What role does the development of renewable sources of energy play in sustainable development?
Answer-
The development of renewable energy sources supports sustainable development by reducing pollution, conserving natural resources, ensuring long-term energy security, improving public health, creating jobs, and providing clean, affordable energy without harming the environment or future generations.

4. How sustainable development protects Earth's biodiversity?
Answer-
Sustainable development protects Earth’s biodiversity by conserving natural habitats, using resources responsibly, reducing pollution, controlling deforestation, and promoting renewable energy. It balances human needs with ecosystem protection, ensuring that plants and animals survive and ecosystems remain stable for future generations.

5. Write any three things you can do to ensure availability of clean water in your environment.
Answer-
1. Use water wisely and avoid wastage in daily activities like bathing, washing, and cleaning.
2. Prevent water pollution by not dumping waste, chemicals, or plastics into water bodies.
3. Practice rainwater harvesting to recharge groundwater and ensure clean water availability.
