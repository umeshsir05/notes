Chapter – 3

Barrier in Communication


1. Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs). Tick() the correct answer.

1. Suppose you are trying to read a book in a room filled with loud noise, what kind of communication barrier to understanding the book content are you facing?
a. Personal
b. Intra-personal
c. Environmental(✓)
d. Inter-personal
Answer - c

2. Which of these is not a common communication barrier?
a. Language
b. Cultural
c. Interpersonal
d. Financial(✓)
Answer - d

3. Which of these does not pose a barrier to communication?
a. Planning(✓)
b. Noise
c. Lack of trust
d. Distance
Answer – a.

4. Which of these is a way to overcome language barrier?
a. Respecting differences
b. Using a translator(✓)
c. Avoiding communication
d. Using simple language
Answer – b.

5. Which of these may help us to overcome a communication barrier?
a. Having clarity in thoughts(✓)
b. Language differences
c. Prejudices and stereotyping
d. Cultural differences
Answer – a.

III. Short Answer Questions:

1.What is the meaning of communication barriers?
Answer -
A communication barrier is anything that prevents a message from being received or understood as intended.
There are many types of communication barriers that can hinder the delivery or understanding of a message.

2. What are environmental barriers to communication? Give two examples.
Answer -
Environmental barriers: Environmental barriers are tangible (physical) or intangible obstacles that prevent effective communication between people. For example,
1.Distance can act as barrier in communication.
2.Noisy surroundings can also act as a barrier to communication. Examples: Loud noise, poor lighting.

3. What are organisational barriers to communication?
Answer -
Organisational barriers happen when rules policies and strict hierarchies make it hard for people to talk freely at work.Employees may feel uncomfortable talking to their seniors or even coworkers.These barriers need to be handled well to improve communication in the organization.

4. List any two measures to overcome the barriers in communication.
Answer -

The two measures to overcome the barriers in communication are-
(i) Use easy and simple language to convey the message.
(ii) Try to communicate in person as much as possible.

5. What do you understand by 7Cs of communication?
Answer-

The 7 Cs of effective communication also known as the seven principles of communication are a useful way to ensure good and effective communication.
They are: Clear, Concise, Concrete, Correct, Coherent, Complete, and Courteous.
