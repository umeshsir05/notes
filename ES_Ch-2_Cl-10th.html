Chapter – 2

Communication Cycle and Importance of Feedback


I. Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs). Tick(✔) the correct answer.
1. Which of these can occur at any stage of the Communication Cycle?
a. Noise
b. Message
c. Feedback
d. Encoding
Answer - a. Noise

2. Which of the following is not a part of the communication cycle?
a. Message
b. Sender
c. Receiver
d. Culture
Answer - d. Culture

3. Which of these is not a type of feedback?
a. Ideal
b. Specific
c. Evaluative
d. Descriptive
Answer - a. Ideal

4. Which type of feedback is more likely to influence our thought process helping us to modify our thoughts and behaviour?
a. Specific feedback
b. Non-specific feedback
c. Summative feedback
d. Formative feedback
Answer - a. Specific feedback

5. Suppose you have made a project. When you show it to your teacher she pats your back and says 'good job.' What type of feedback is this?
a. Specific
b. Non-specific
c. Evaluative
d. None of these
Answer - b. Non-specific

6. Which of these are examples of negative feedback?
a. I hate to tell you this but your communication skills are really poor.
b. You can surely improve your communication skills.
c. Your communication skills are good but surely can do better with practice.
d. None of the above
Answer - a. I hate to tell you this but your communication skills are really poor.

III. Short Answer Questions.

1. Briefly explain the communication cycle.
Answer -
The communication cycle is the process of exchanging information or a message between a sender and a receiver, using both verbal and nonverbal methods. It begins with the sender, who decides the message, encoding, channel, receiver, decoding, and ends with feedback. It has seven elements: Sender, Message, Encoding, Channel, Receiver, Decoding, and Feedback.
