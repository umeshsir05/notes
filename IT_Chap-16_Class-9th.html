IV. Short Answer Questions.

1. What is a Presentation?
Answer -
A presentation can be defined as an act of showing and explaining a message, Idea, or a topic to an audience with the help of text, voice, graphics, charts, videos and/or animation etc., usually on a big screen with the help of a projector.

2. Write any five characteristics of a good presentation.
Answer -
Here are 5 characteristics of of a good presentation -
i. Keep information limited and to the point: Do not try to include too much information in the presentation. Stick to three or four points about your topic and elaborate on them.
ii. Use minimal text: On a slide try to include not more than 5 to 6 lines. Adding more lines of text on a slide will make it difficult for the audience to read and comprehend the essence of the presentation.
iii. Use less number of slides: Ideally, your presentation should not be too long. It should have 10 or fewer slides.
iv. Use appropriate Design Template or theme: Choose a design that is appropriate for the topic and the targeted audience. A clean, straight-forward layout is ideal for business presentations.
v. Choose colours wisely: Good contrast with the background is essential to make your text easy to read. Dark text on a light background is best.

3. What is presentation software? Explain by giving examples.
Answer -
A presentation software is a special type of application program that allows us to create presentations by combining text, pictures, movie & sound clips, animation, charts, graphics and other multimedia tools.

4. What are the basic elements of a presentation? Give a brief description of each element.
Answer -
The basic elements of a presentation are-
1.Title and Subtitle text.
2.Bulleted Text and plain text in text boxes.
3.Images, Pictures, Graphics, and Charts etc.
4.Speakers Notes and Narration.
5.Sound, Video and Animation.
6.Header and Footer, Shapes, designer text and drawings
7.Hyperlinks, transitions, animation effects etc.

5. What is a slide?
Answer -
We know that a PowerPoint presentation is made up of slides. A slide therefore is the most basic element of a presentation.

6. Write in brief about different types of presentation views.
Answer -
1.Normal View- The Normal view is the main view for creating individual slides. Use this view to format and design slides and to add text, graphics, and animation effects.
2.Outline View- The Outline view displays all the slides of the presentation in a numbered sequence. It shows topic titles, bulleted lists, and numbered lists for each slide in outline format.
3.Notes View- Use the Notes view to add speaker's notes to a slide. These notes are not visible when the presentation is shown.
4.Slide Sorter View- The Slide Sorter view is the thumbnail view of all the slides in a presentation. 5.Slide Show View- The Slide Show view is the full screen view in which you can see your presentation the way your audience will see it.

7. What is the use of slide sorter view?
Answer -
Slide Sorter View displays all slides as thumbnails. Best for reordering slides, deleting unnecessary ones, or checking the overall sequence of the presentation.

8. Describe the zoom controls which are there in the Impress Window?
Answer -
The Zoom Control slider is used to change the zoom strength of an active slide. Dragging the slider towards right increases the size of the active slide and dragging it toward left decreases the slide size.

9. What is the extension of Impress presentation files?
Answer -
The default extension of LibreOffice Impress presentation files is .odp(OpenDocument Presentation).It is the standard format used by LibreOffice Impress.

10. How will you delete a slide from a presentation?
Answer -
Select slide that you want to delete and press the delete key.

11. How will you run a slide show from the currently selected slide?
Answer -
Shift+F5 keys will start it from the currently selected slide.

12. Which view is used to show just a single slide with all its contents?
Answer -
Normal View shows one slide at a time. It displays all contents of that slide (text, images, charts, etc.)This is the main view used for creating and editing slides.

13. How will you perform Undo and Redo operations in Impress?
Answer -
Undo operation-
1. To undo a mistake click the Undo button on the Standard toolbar.
2. You can also press the Ctrl + Z keys to undo a mistake.
Redo operation-
1. To redo an action undone by the Undo command, click on the Redo button on the Standard toolbar.
2. You can also press the Ctrl + Y keys to redo the undone action.


14. Rama is preparing a presentation on her company's annual performance. She wants to include a few pictures from the LibreOffice Gallery. How can she do it? List all the required steps.
Answer -
The exact steps Rama should follow in LibreOffice Impress to insert images from the Gallery.
1. Click to open the Insert menu.
2. Select the Media option. A submenu will open.
3. Select Gallery option from the submenu. This will open up the Gallery panel towards the right side in the Impress window.
4. Select a theme of images from the list of themes displayed in the Gallery panel.
5. Select an image and right click on it.
6. Choose Insert option from the shortcut menu.


15. What is Zoom Slider?
Answer -
Zoom Slider is a tool in LibreOffice Impress that lets you increase or decrease the zoom level of the slide.

    a. To view all slides together
    b. To change the order of the slides
    c. To show the sales using a chart

c. To show the sales using a chart-Chart (Insert → Chart) Used to present sales data visually (bar chart, pie chart, line chart, etc.).

17. What is the slide master? What is the main advantage of using slide master in a presentation?
Answer -
A Slide Master or Master Slide is a special type of slide that stores information about the template and slide layouts of a presentation including the background, color, fonts, effects, placeholder sizes, positioning and so on.

18. What is the use of image formatting in Impress?
Answer -
Image formatting in LibreOffice Impress is used to adjust the size, position, orientation, and appearance of images—such as cropping, rotating, and applying effects—so that slides look clear, attractive, and professionally presented.

19. What is the content placeholder?
Answer -
Various types of slide contents are generally placed inside the placeholders, which are small rectangular boxes on a slide with dotted outline.

To insert duplicate slide follow the steps given below:
1. Select the Slide in the Slides pane whose duplicate you want to insert.
2. Click on the Slide menu → Duplicate Slide option.
... A duplicate slide will be added after the selected slide.
